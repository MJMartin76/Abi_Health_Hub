üíö Personal Care Guide

Important: These are step-by-step instructions for daily hygiene. Follow this guide every day. Personal care is not optional - it's part of staying healthy!

üåÖ Daily Morning Hygiene Routine

  1. Wake up gently - take 3 deep breaths
  2. Sit up slowly, stretch
  3. Put on compression socks FIRST (doctor's orders)
  4. Use bathroom
  5. Wash face with gentle cleanser, apply moisturizer
  6. Brush teeth for 2 minutes (use timer)
  7. Comb hair, pull back from face
  8. Get dressed in clean clothes
  9. Apply deodorant to BOTH underarms
  10. Drink 16 oz water with lemon

üöø Shower Routine (Use Shower Stool)

How often: Minimum every other day (Mon/Wed/Fri/Sun), better every day

  1. Gather supplies and place shower stool
  2. Sit on shower stool safely
  3. Wet entire body including hair
  4. Shampoo: Quarter-sized amount, massage scalp, rinse thoroughly
  5. Condition: Apply mid-length to ends, let sit 1-2 min, rinse
  6. Wash body: neck/chest ‚Üí underarms (scrub well!) ‚Üí arms ‚Üí belly/back ‚Üí private areas ‚Üí legs ‚Üí feet
  7. Rinse entire body thoroughly
  8. Dry off completely (including between toes)
  9. Apply moisturizer, put on clean clothes
  10. Apply deodorant, comb hair

üöΩ Bathroom Hygiene

After urinating:

Use 3-4 squares toilet paper (folded), wipe front to back, total 6-8 squares max, flush, wash hands for 20 seconds.

After bowel movement:

Use 4-5 squares (folded), wipe front to back, repeat if needed (2-3 wipes usually), total 10-15 squares max, use flushable wipe for final cleaning, flush, wash hands thoroughly.

üò¥ Evening Hygiene Routine

  1. Use bathroom
  2. Brush teeth for 2 minutes, floss, use mouthwash
  3. Wash face with cleanser, apply night moisturizer
  4. Wash feet (warm water, soap, dry completely)
  5. Apply thick moisturizer to feet, put on clean socks
  6. Comb hair, secure for sleeping
  7. Remove compression socks, set out for morning

üëï When to Change Clothes

  • Underwear: Change EVERY day
  • Socks: Change EVERY day
  • Shirts: Change every 1-2 days or if sweaty/dirty
  • Pants: Can wear 2-3 days if clean
  • Pajamas: Change every 2-3 days
  • Rule: If you're not sure if clothes are clean, they're NOT clean - change them

üìö Understanding My Health

üò¥ Chronic Fatigue

  • Your body needs structured rest (6 hours lying down) AND structured activity
  • Lying in bed all day makes fatigue WORSE, not better
  • Regular movement actually improves energy over time
  • Balance is key: rest when scheduled, stay active otherwise

üî¨ PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)

  • A hormonal condition affecting ovaries, insulin, and metabolism
  • Exercise helps: Regular movement improves insulin sensitivity
  • Diet matters: Low glycemic foods prevent blood sugar spikes
  • Regular routine helps: Consistent meal/sleep times support hormones
  • No eating after 6 PM: Helps regulate hormones and improve sleep

ü¶∂ Gout

  • Caused by uric acid buildup in joints, causes pain and inflammation
  • Diet is KEY: Avoid high-purine foods (red meat, certain seafood)
  • Hydration is CRITICAL: 80-100 oz water daily flushes uric acid
  • Movement helps: Gentle exercise improves circulation
  • Tremors: Related to gout inflammation and nervous system response

üß† Trauma Response & Nervous System Retraining

  • Your brain learned "movement = pain" when gout was severe
  • This created a protective fear response
  • Your body sometimes trembles as a nervous system response
  • You're retraining: PT, speech, and OT teach your brain movement is safe
  • This is TEMPORARY: With consistent practice, your nervous system will calm
  • You are NOT broken: Your body was protecting you; now you're teaching it to trust
  • Every exercise session is PROOF: Movement doesn't cause pain anymore. You're safe.

ü©∫ Signs of Gout Flare

  • Sudden intense joint pain (often in big toe)
  • Swelling in the joint
  • Redness and warmth in affected area
  • Limited range of motion
  • Tenderness - even light touch hurts

If you notice these signs, tell Mindy immediately! Gout flares need quick treatment.

üß† Managing Tremors

Remember: Your tremors are a nervous system response, not danger.

When tremors start:

  1. Pause what you're doing
  2. Take slow, deep breaths (box breathing: 4-4-4-4)
  3. Sit down if standing
  4. Tell yourself: "This is just nervous energy. I am safe."
  5. Slow down or take a short break
  6. Continue when tremors calm down

Tell Mindy if: Tremors don't calm with breathing, or are worse than usual.
